# Josiah Couch

I am a postdoctoral research fellow in the [Arnaout Lab](https://arnaoutlab.org/) at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, where I work on machine learning, with a focus on medical imaging and computational immunology.
My research sits at the intersection of information theory, physics, and machine learning. Recent work has focused on developing entropy based diversity measures originating from ecology into tools for evaluating and curating machine learning datasets. I also maintain an active interest in quantum gravity, particularly the AdS/CFT correspondence and its connections to quantum information theory.
I earned my Ph.D. in physics from the University of Texas at Austin in 2021, where I worked with the [Weinberg Theory Group](https://zippy.ph.utexas.edu/) on holographic complexity.
